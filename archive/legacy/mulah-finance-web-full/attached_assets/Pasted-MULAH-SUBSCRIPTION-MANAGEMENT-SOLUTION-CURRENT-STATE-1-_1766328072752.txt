MULAH — SUBSCRIPTION MANAGEMENT SOLUTION (CURRENT STATE)

1. Core Problem Mulah Solves

Modern subscriptions fail users because:
	•	billing dates are fragmented and unpredictable
	•	money is spread across multiple accounts
	•	merchants control billing logic, not users
	•	existing tools only track or buffer subscriptions, they don’t control them

Mulah’s goal is to give users real control over subscriptions, not just visibility.

⸻

2. Mulah’s Positioning (Very Important)

Mulah is not:
	•	a savings pot
	•	a passive subscription tracker
	•	a wallet holding customer funds
	•	a merchant of record (in V1)

Mulah is:
	•	a subscription billing control layer
	•	a liquidity orchestration system
	•	a unified subscription wallet (USW)
	•	a future subscription identity infrastructure

⸻

3. The Three Core Layers of Mulah

Layer 1 — Subscription Billing Authority

This layer decides:
	•	when a subscription is due
	•	how much is due
	•	what rules apply (proration, retries, pauses)

This is where billing-date control lives.

⸻

Layer 2 — Unified Subscription Wallet (USW)

USW:
	•	aggregates all subscriptions (onboarded + non-onboarded)
	•	provides one user-facing control surface
	•	schedules upcoming obligations
	•	enforces user preferences (billing date, limits, rules)
	•	integrates with CFA for forecasting and insights

USW does not hold money.
It schedules and coordinates obligations.

⸻

Layer 3 — Liquidity Orchestration Engine (LOE)

LOE answers only one question:

“How do we fund this obligation safely, compliantly, and optimally?”

LOE:
	•	reads balances across multiple accounts (AISP)
	•	builds a funding plan
	•	initiates authorised debits (PISP)
	•	can split payments across accounts
	•	never pools or stores funds
	•	never lends money in V1

⸻

4. Two Active Subscription Control Methods

Method 1 — Cooperative Merchant Billing Control

Who it’s for:
Merchants who onboard to Mulah (gyms, SaaS, services, SMEs).

What Mulah controls:
	•	billing date
	•	billing cycle
	•	proration
	•	retries
	•	pauses / resumes
	•	upgrades / downgrades

How it works:
	•	Merchant uses Mulah as their subscription engine
	•	User selects preferred billing date
	•	Mulah enforces alignment directly
	•	LOE funds the charge at execution time

Result:
	•	True billing-date control
	•	Lower churn for merchants
	•	One unified billing experience for users

⸻

Method 3 — Subscription Identity Layer (Mulah Billing Token)

Who it’s for:
Merchants willing to delegate billing identity (long-term play).

What changes:
	•	Merchant no longer stores card/bank details
	•	Merchant stores a Mulah Billing Token
	•	Mulah defines billing date and cycle

Flow:
	1.	User subscribes using “Subscribe with Mulah”
	2.	Merchant charges Mulah token
	3.	Mulah validates the request
	4.	LOE sources funds from user accounts
	5.	Merchant is settled cleanly

Result:
	•	Mulah controls billing dates globally
	•	Strong network effects
	•	Mulah becomes subscription infrastructure

⸻

5. What Happens with Big / Non-Cooperative Merchants

Examples: Netflix, Disney+, Tinder, Apple/Google-billed subscriptions.

Mulah cannot:
	•	change their billing dates
	•	override platform billing logic

Mulah can:
	•	recognise and track them
	•	forecast obligations
	•	prevent failures via liquidity orchestration
	•	guide cancellation or switching to direct billing if available

These are classified as:

Non-Controllable Subscriptions (NCS)

Mulah is honest about this distinction.

⸻

6. Billing Date Reality (Clear Rule)
	•	Billing dates are only changed where Mulah has billing authority
	•	Method 1 (merchant onboarded)
	•	Method 3 (Mulah billing token)
	•	Otherwise, Mulah stabilises outcomes but does not falsely claim control

This preserves trust and compliance.

⸻

7. Liquidity Orchestration (Key Innovation)

Mulah eliminates the problem of fragmented money by:
	•	treating all authorised accounts as one liquidity pool
	•	executing payments from multiple accounts if needed
	•	enforcing user-defined rules (min balances, exclusions)

This allows:
	•	subscriptions to succeed even if no single account has enough
	•	users to stop thinking in “which account should I pay from?”

⸻

8. Regulatory Posture (V1)

Mulah operates as:
	•	AISP (account visibility)
	•	PISP (payment initiation)
	•	billing coordinator

Mulah does not:
	•	hold customer funds
	•	act as a bank
	•	act as a lender
	•	act as merchant of record

This keeps V1 low-risk and fast to market.

⸻

9. Failure & Safety Principles
	•	No partial execution without compensation
	•	No negative balances
	•	No implicit credit
	•	No silent failures
	•	Full auditability of consent and actions

⸻

10. What Makes This Genuinely Innovative

Mulah is not just managing subscriptions — it is re-architecting how subscriptions work:
	•	Billing dates become user-controlled (where possible)
	•	Liquidity is orchestrated across accounts in real time
	•	Subscriptions become deterministic, not chaotic
	•	Merchants gain lower churn
	•	Users gain predictability and calm

This is infrastructure, not a feature.

⸻

11. Current State Summary (One Sentence)

Mulah is a consent-driven subscription billing and liquidity orchestration platform that gives users real control over when subscriptions are billed and how they are funded—without Mulah ever holding or owning their money
